Entreprenurship Greenhouse

PROBLEM

Representing the Design Clinic at the School of Information, I along with 3 other group members worked as UX consultants and designers to design the User Experience and the User Interface of the pilot phase for the Entrepreneurship Greenhouse website.

TEAM

Anay Somvanshi,Chaitrali Gharat,Kanchi Bhawalkar & Swarnima Deshmukh

TIMELINE

Jan 2016 - Ongoing

BACKGROUND

Various Innovation and Entrepreneurship programs housed across the Law School, Ross School of Business, School of Engineering & School of Public Health at the University of Michigan partnered with the Digital Education and Innovation Group to start an initiative called as the Entrepreneurship Greenhouse.

As part of this initiative, they envisioned a website which would house videos that would help budding entrepreneurs get started with their startup ventures be it choosing a name for their brand or helping them with Trademark infringement.

FOCUS GROUPS

The client conducted interviews with focus groups from across the university...




...and came up with the information architecture for the website.


INFORMATION ARCHITECHTURE

For the pilot phase, this information flow would be comprised of 9 videos and four questions.

PROBLEM STATEMENT

- We met with the client to at this stage and were assigned with the problem statement. From the meeting we understood that we needed to translate this flowchart into a 'bitesized' intuitive and quickly navigable website . The key phrases we decided to focus on before starring out on our design were :

  • 'Make your own adventure'
  • Non traditional Learning Management System
  • 'One time visit website'
  • 'great navigation experience'

Based upon the above prompt, the design challenge could be divided into two main parts:

COMPARATIVE ANALYSIS

For this particular project, the comparative analysis consisted more of drawing inspiration from various MOOCs and video sharing platforms to find the little things they were doing best and trying to find inspiration for our design for both our sections.


IDEATION

We then proceeded to ideate and brainstorm depending upon our interpretations of the design prompt couple with the inspirations we drew from our comparative analysis.

WIREFRAMES

Depending on the ideas, we created numerous wireframes, each different from the other.

STYLE GUIDE & HI-FI PROTOTYPES

We then met with the client to decide upon the navigation style and then proceed to make numerous Hifi prototypes as well. We also decided upon the style guide and color scheme for the website

We are currently in the process of iterating over the navigation style we decided upon.
